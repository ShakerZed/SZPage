What I Believe
The mat and the office are the same room
Combat sports taught me jita kyoei: mutual welfare and mutual benefit. In the right culture, everyone gives to each other and everyone grows because of it. A training partner who levels up forces you to level up. A teammate who develops makes the whole unit more capable. I build environments where that exchange is the default, not the exception.
Alignment compounds, misalignment kills
The best techniques fail without timing. The best strategies fail without buy-in. I've learned that getting a team genuinely pointed in the same direction is more valuable than any individual contribution. Everything I build starts with making sure we agree on where we're going.
Never stop being a white belt
Choosing to learn something new is choosing to climb a mountain you can see but haven't touched. I learned shell voicings to hear what makes math rock tick beneath the surface. I study the dilemma in judo and jiu-jitsu, how forcing an opponent to solve two problems at once creates openings, and I watch the same principle play out in negotiations, product positioning, and team dynamics. The hunger to dig into the nuances of unfamiliar terrain is the only edge that doesn't expire.
